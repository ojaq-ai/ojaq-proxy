What happens here

One room. One voice that listens. Many ways to be met.

You arrive. A concierge greets you — one voice, listening for what's alive in what you bring. No menus to navigate, no settings to choose. Just a short exchange about what you need.

From there, you're taken to the room that fits — coaching when you're untangling something, a mirror when you need to be seen, a friend when you just want to talk, a practice when you want to settle, a witness when there are two of you.

When it speaks back, it's rarely and precisely. One sentence. Two at most. Only reflections so accurate they feel uncanny.
